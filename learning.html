Professional Skills Matrix and action plan (PDP)

Never give up. It is the world's simplest cop-out. Make a goal and stick with it until you achieve it. When you succeed in achieving it, set another one and keep going until you do. Never give up. - Bear Bryant

What do I hope to become after finishing my course?

As an IT professional, my goal is to keep up with the necessary IT Tech requirements while monitoring the performance of IT systems. It is my responsibility to look into the market prices for software and IT supplies and to keep track of all of these things for the times when I will need to use them in my projects. I currently wish to succeed as an IT Project Manager.

Goals and Outcomes

Short-term and Long-term Objectives

● Project Planning: Attend a two-day seminar on advanced project planning techniques during the next six months. Use these strategies in practise on next projects.

● Risk Management: I will ask to be in charge of a high-risk project within the next year so I can learn from the team's skilled risk managers. Additionally, read two risk management books over the subsequent six months.

● Stakeholder Management: For the subsequent project, create a plan for including stakeholders, and ask for their input at least twice. Within the next year, enrol in a stakeholder management training programme.

● Communication: Every three months, ask team members and stakeholders for input on my communication techniques. In the upcoming six months, enrol in a public speaking course as well.

● Leadership: Enroll in a three-day leadership development course within the following year. Take on a leadership role in a cross-functional team during the next six months.

● Budget Management: Within the next year, ask to manage a more complicated project budget. Attend a budget management course as well within the following six months.

● Change Management: Attend a two-day change management programme within the next year. Offer to lead a company-wide reform initiative during the next six months.
